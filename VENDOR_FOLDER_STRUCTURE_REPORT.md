## Assumptions Made

1. **Categorization is name-based only.** No invoice content was inspected. Categories were inferred from vendor name keywords (e.g. "Plumbing", "Heating", "Pest Control", "Electric"). Where a name contained multiple plausible service lines (e.g. "Plumbing Heating Cooling and Electrical"), the first matching pattern in the priority order was used.

2. **Two extra categories were added beyond the user's example list:**
   - **`Individuals - Contractors`** — for vendors whose names look like personal names (2–4 words, no business indicator like LLC/Inc/Corp/Services, no all-caps acronym). Many of these are likely 1099 sub-contractors, individual property owners, or tenants receiving refunds; the actual service line cannot be inferred from a personal name alone.
   - **`Owners - Investors - Holdings`** — for entities whose names indicate property ownership / investment (e.g. "Holdings", "Investments", "Ventures", "Fund", "Trust", "Estate", "Partners", "Apartments LLC", "LP"). These are typically not service vendors but capital-side counterparties.

3. **Combined utility names go to the dominant service.** For example "Clarksville Gas and Water" was placed under **Water - Sewer** (water service is the dominant billing line for property mgmt), and "Pulaski Natural Gas & Water" likewise. "Columbia Power and Water System" went to **Electricity - Power**. Final routing should be re-verified once an actual invoice is available.

4. **Public utility ↔ contractor disambiguation.** Names matching a known utility list (e.g. *Alabama Power*, *CDE Lightband*, *Kentucky Utilities*) were routed to **Electricity - Power**. Other vendors with "Electric" in the name (e.g. *Fox Electric LLC*, *CAM Electric*) were routed to **Electrical Contractors**. The exclusion list was hand-curated; if a small electric coop is missing it would land in Electrical Contractors instead.

5. **"Petty Cash" entries are vendors per the source CSV** and were given subfolders under **Property Management - Petty Cash**. They represent property-level cash floats, not third-party vendors, but the source data treats them as vendor records, so they are preserved as such.

6. **`HD Supply` lines were treated as Building Supplies** even when the name includes "Facilities Maintenance", because HD Supply is a building/MRO distributor.
